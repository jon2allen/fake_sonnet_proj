**Sonnet CLIV**

The wanton boy, with drowsy eyelids sealed,
Left by his side the torch that sets hearts aflame,
While maidens chaste, their vows of virtue steeled,
Passed lightly by—yet one, untouched by shame,
Stooped low and took the brand that long had burned
In loyal breasts, where Cupid’s arrows play.
Thus was the god of love by hand outturned,
His power undone by one who would not stay.
She plunged the fire in waters dark and deep,
Where Love’s own heat did turn the tide to steam,
A bath for those who sigh in fevered sleep,
Yet vain the cure for passion’s endless dream.
For though the wave may quench the outward blaze,
No flood can drown the fire that love betrays.
