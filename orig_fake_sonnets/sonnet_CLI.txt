**Sonnet CLI**

O love, thou art a child with conscience blind,
Yet conscience springs where love hath taken root.
Then mock me not, nor chide my fault unkind,
Lest thou, too sweet, be tangled in my fruit.
For if thou play the traitor to my trust,
I sell my soul to flesh’s base deceit;
My spirit cries thee lord, though reason’s dust
Yields to thy name, and all my thoughts retreat.
Lo, at thy call my body stands and bows,
Proud as a king who claims his conquered prize;
It scorns the soul’s faint plea, and thee avows,
To serve thy will, though death in thine eyes lies.
 Yet call I not this sin, but love’s own art,
 For whom my soul doth rise, my flesh doth part.
