**Sonnet LXXXIII**

O, why should art presume to gild thy name,
When nature’s hand hath writ thee fairest gold?
No borrowed hues could heighten half thy flame,
Nor cunning verse thy living grace unfold.
I, mute, beheld thee, and in silence found
A truth no tongue could match, no pen could trace—
For who could paint the light wherewith thee crowned,
Or frame the fire that doth thy soul embrace?

Yet some there be who, with laborious art,
Would carve thee cold in marble, pale and dead,
Or chain thy spirit in their feeble part,
And call thee theirs when thou art fled and fled.
 But lo! thy glance doth breathe a purer air
 Than all their quills could conjure, though they swear.
