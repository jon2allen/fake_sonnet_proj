**Sonnet CXXIV**

O love, not bred of fortune’s fleeting breath,
Nor nursed where fickle favor holds its sway,
That blooms to wither, or survives decay,
A plaything tossed by time or chance or death.
No gilded chain nor hollow pomp ensnares,
Nor storms of envy shake thy steadfast root,
Nor policy, that serpent with smooth suit,
Can mar the truth thy constant heart declares.

Thou stand’st alone, unbowed by fate’s design,
Unmoved by praise or scorn, by sun or rain,
A tower unshaken by the winds of years.
No lease of hours can bind what is divine—
For time may mock, yet never touch thy reign,
Nor fools who trade their souls for fleeting cheers.
