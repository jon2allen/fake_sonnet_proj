**Sonnet XXVI**

O sovereign of my soul, to whom I bow
In fealty deep, where love doth bind me fast,
This humble scroll I send, not to avow
My cunning art, but duty’s steadfast cast.
For what are words when weighed ‘gainst thy desert?
My tongue, too weak, may falter in the test,
Yet trust I that thy noble, gracious heart
Shall clothe my bare devotion in thy breast.

Till fortune’s star, that guides my wandering way,
Shall deign to smile upon my lowly state,
And gild my ragged love with purest ray,
That I may stand approved by thy high fate:
   Then shall I dare to speak my heart’s true measure;
   Till then, I wait, nor seek to claim thy treasure.
