**Sonnet XL**

O take, my heart, what more remains to take?
For thou hast all—what boots it thee to crave?
No truer love thy hollow hands may make,
Since all was thine ere thou didst seek to save.
Yet if my love thou dost in love embrace,
No fault is thine, for love doth love pursue;
But fault is thine if thou dost play false grace,
And taste the fruit thou once didst bid adieu.
I pardon thee, sweet thief of all my store,
Though thou dost leave me naught but empty air;
Yet love’s deep wound doth grieve me more and more
Than hate’s sharp sting, for hate is plain and fair.
O cruel kind, in whom all ill seems sweet,
Slay me with scorn, yet let our bond not fleet.
