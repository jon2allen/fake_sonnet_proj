**Sonnet LXVI**

Weary of all, I sigh for death’s release,
To see true worth in rags and want confined,
While hollow fools in gilded chains find peace,
And faith, once bright, is by false vows maligned.
Behold how honour, decked in tinsel show,
Is cast on knaves who scorn its weight and grace,
While virtue, pure as morning’s earliest glow,
Is dragged through mire by some unchaste embrace.
The strong are bowed by sway of feeble hands,
And art, once free, is chained by tyrant’s law,
While witless men, in scholar’s robes arrayed,
Command the wise with folly’s haughty awe.
 Yet from this world I dare not flee too soon,
 Lest in my death I leave my love alone.
