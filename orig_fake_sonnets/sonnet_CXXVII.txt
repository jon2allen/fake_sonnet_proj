**Sonnet CXXVII**

When golden locks were crown’d with beauty’s grace,
And ivory brows did hold the world’s delight,
No dusky hue could claim a rightful place,
Nor dare to stand where fairest shone so bright.
Yet now the false hath stolen the true’s renown,
With painted cheeks and borrowed sunlit gleam,
While Nature’s art, unspoil’d, is cast adown,
And truth doth weep beneath a gilded dream.

But lo! my love’s dark eyes, like midnight’s star,
Outshine the counterfeit of paler hue,
For they, in mourning for the wrongs they mar,
Do scorn the theft that makes the false seem true.
   So let them weep, for in their sorrow’s night,
   They prove what beauty is—pure, deep, and right.
