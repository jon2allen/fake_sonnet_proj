**Sonnet LXX**

O faultless form, let slander’s breath assail,
For envy’s dart but grazes purest gold;
The rose, though fair, must bear the thorn’s harsh toll,
And heaven’s own light is oft by shadows pale.
Yet mark thee well—no stain thy worth can stale,
For time, that rusts the steel, thy grace doth hold;
The lily, though besmirched by mire untold,
Still lifts its head where lesser blooms would fail.

Thou hast outrun the snares of fleeting youth,
Unscathed or crowned in honor’s bright array;
Yet praise too loud might wake the sleeping truth—
For malice thrives where merit lights the way.
If none dared doubt, then none might love thee so—
Thus kings and hearts to thee their tribute owe.
