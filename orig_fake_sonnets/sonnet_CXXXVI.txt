**Sonnet CXXXVI**

If thou dost chide me that I dare draw nigh,
Swear to thy doubting heart I am thy *Love*,
And love, thou know’st, is ever welcome high—
So let my plea in mercy rise above.
*Love* shall thy coffers fill with sweet delight,
Ay, brim thy soul with loves till mine alone
Stands bright amidst the thronging stars of night,
Yet lost where countless treasures brightly shone.
Then count me not, though I must count for one,
A fleeting shade where golden beams do play;
Hold me as naught, yet hold me, dearest sun,
And naught shall be a jewel in thy day.
   Call but my name thy heart’s eternal flame,
   And thou dost love me—for my name’s the same.
