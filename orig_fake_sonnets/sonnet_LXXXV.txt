**Sonnet LXXXV**

My silent quill doth pause in reverent awe,
While others weave thy glory, bright and fair,
With golden lines that time shall ne’er outdraw,
And words that breathe like incense on the air.
I, mute, stand by, though thoughts like stars arise,
And in my breast a thousand praises burn;
Yet when the skilled their polished hymns devise,
I only nod and let my heart discern.

“’Tis true,” I say, “and more than words can tell,”
For love, though dumb, doth speak in deeper part;
No gilded phrase my simple truth may spell,
Yet beats it strong in every throbbing heart.
 Then let them praise with eloquence so fine—
 I’ll love thee best where no words intertwine.
