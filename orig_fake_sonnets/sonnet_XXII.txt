**Sonnet XXII**

My glass may lie, yet time will not deceive,
While thou dost stand where youth and grace entwine;
But when thy brow doth bear the frost’s cold grieve,
Then shall I know my fleeting sun must shine.
For all thy fairness is but love’s own hue,
A garment spun from threads my soul hath wove,
And in thy breast my heart doth beat anew,
As thine in mine doth breathe the breath of love.
Then guard thee well, as I shall guard thee too,
Not for my sake, but thine own heart’s sweet claim;
For thou dost bear what I dare not undo,
A flame too bright to suffer time’s dark flame.
   Yet if my heart should falter, fail, or fade,
   Thine was not lent to be in death repaid.
