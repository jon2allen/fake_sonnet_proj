**Sonnet CXXXVII**

O cruel deceit, Love, what spell dost thou weave,
That binds mine eyes to sights they dare not name?
They mark the grace where no true grace doth live,
And call the dross of folly purest flame.
If sight, by partial lust made blind and base,
Now anchors where all wandering vessels moor,
Why dost thou forge such chains of dark disgrace,
To bind my heart where reason holds no store?

Why should my soul claim some rare, hallowed ground,
When all the world may tread that common way?
Or why should eyes, that see the truth profound,
Deny the fraud and call the falsehood gay?
 In error deep my heart and eyes conspire,
 And feed this plague with fuel of vain desire.
