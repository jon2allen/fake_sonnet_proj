**Sonnet L**

O leaden steps that drag me from thy sight,
Where every league but lengthens love’s despair,
The road, once bright with hope’s unwearied air,
Now mocks my heart with distance, cold and white.
The steed that bears me, dull with sorrow’s blight,
Moves slow as though he bore the weight of care,
Nor whip nor spur can bid his footsteps fare—
He knows too well my soul takes no delight.

What boots it then to urge him with the rein?
His groans are echoes of my own despair,
More keen than lash, more sharp than goad or pain,
For in his sighs I hear the bitter air
That whispers, *Lo, thy joy lies far behind,*
*And grief alone walks with thee on the wind.*
