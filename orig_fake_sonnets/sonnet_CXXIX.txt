**Sonnet CXXIX**

O fleeting fire that burns the soul to dust,
A moment’s bliss, a lifetime’s bitter cost—
No sooner kindled than in ashes tossed,
No sooner held than in the wind’s rude thrust.
It lures with honeyed lips and honeyed lies,
A serpent’s kiss, a thief that steals the breath,
A fevered dream where waking only dies,
A hell disguised in robes of fleeting death.
The heart, once bound, now chains itself in vain,
For love’s sweet trap is but a gilded snare;
The more it grasps, the more it feeds the pain,
And all its gold turns leaden in the air.
 Yet still we chase the flame we know will fade,
 And trade the stars for embers in the shade.
