**Sonnet X**

O cruel! Deny not love’s sweet claim to thee,
Who scorns the gift thou ow’st thine own fair frame.
Though many sigh and call thy name with glee,
Thou spurn’st them all—thy heart a barren flame.
For lo! Thy soul, with venom’s blackest art,
Doth plot against the temple thou shouldst prize,
Seeking to mar the beauty of thy part,
When thou alone couldst heal its bright disguise.

Ah, turn thy thought, that mine may turn as well!
Shall hate outshine the grace of love’s pure light?
Be as thy mien—a heaven where angels dwell,
Or show thyself at least some mercy’s sight.
 For love of me, let kindness shape thy way,
 That beauty live in thee, or thee display.
