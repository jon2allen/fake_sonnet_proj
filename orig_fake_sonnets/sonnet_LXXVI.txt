**Sonnet LXXVI**

Why dost my quill, so bound in steadfast rhyme,
Refuse the dance of fleeting fashions bright?
Why scorn I not the gilded toys of time,
Nor chase the shadows of each borrowed light?
I write, yet write again the selfsame lay,
As though my muse in one poor garb were clad,
And every line doth bear my heart’s array,
A map of love no shifting winds have swayed.
Ah! Know’st thou not, dear soul, ‘tis thee I sing,
And thee alone my constant verse doth trace?
Though words may tire, yet love doth freshly spring,
And dress old thoughts in ever-new embrace.
 For as the dawn doth kiss the fading night,
 So love renews what time would bid take flight.
