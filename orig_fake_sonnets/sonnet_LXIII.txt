**Sonnet LXIII**

When time hath laid his leaden hand on thee,
And carved with care each furrow in thy face,
When golden locks, once bright as morning’s grace,
Shall fade to silver, thin as winter’s tree—
Then shall my verse thy vanished youth decree,
And bid thy beauty mock the fleeting space;
For though thy limbs may bow to time’s embrace,
My lines shall hold thee in eternity.

O cruelest thief! That steals what love hath prized,
Yet cannot touch the treasure I have penned;
Though age may wither what mine eyes have seen,
These words shall bloom where mortal flesh has died.
So live, my love, in ink’s unyielding sheen,
When all the world else fades to dust between.
