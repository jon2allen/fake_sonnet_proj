**Sonnet XCIII**

Thus must I dwell, suspecting naught of thee,
As one who walks in shadows, blind yet bright,
For love’s fair mask still smiles, though secretly
Thy soul hath fled to some unlooked-for light.
No storm clouds gather in thine eyes’ clear sky,
No tremor mars the calm thy lips impart—
Yet in thy breast, what hidden fires may lie,
What whispered vows betray thy faithless heart?

O fate too cruel! To carve in beauty’s frame
A heaven of grace, yet leave the soul untrue,
That outward show should bear no guilt nor blame,
While inward thoughts conspire to work me woe.
 Like golden fruit with rotten core below,
 Thy face is sweet—yet who shall taste and know?
