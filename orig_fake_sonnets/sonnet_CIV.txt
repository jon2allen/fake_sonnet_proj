**Sonnet CIV**

To thee, bright star, no frost of time shall cling,
For in thine eye the dawn yet breaks anew,
Though seasons turn and autumn’s gold undo
The bloom that once did on thy springtime sing.
Three winters’ breath hath chilled the forest’s wing,
Yet still thy cheek outshines the morning’s hue,
As though the sun, in mercy, bid adieu
To all but thee, fair thief of time’s own sting.

O fleeting grace! Like shadows on the sand,
Thou steal’st unseen, yet leave’st thy mark behind—
A ghost of youth that mocks my trembling hand.
So doth thy light, though constant to my mind,
Betray the truth: that beauty, frail and grand,
Must fade ere time itself dare call thee blind.
