**Sonnet CXXII**

Not parchment frail nor waxen tablet’s art
Shall hold thee fast as doth this steadfast mind,
Where love’s deep graven lines no time can part,
Nor fate’s unkindest hand erase or bind.
For what are scrolls but shadows of the soul?
What ink but faintest echo of the heart?
No outward mark could half thy worth enroll,
Nor mortal hand thy endless grace impart.

Yet once I thought some trinket might suffice—
A bauble to enshrine thy fleeting light—
Till reason scorned such folly, cold as ice,
And bade me trust the fire that burns more bright.
 To clasp a relic while thy spirit flies
 Were but to chain the sun in painted skies.
