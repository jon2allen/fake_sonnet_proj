**Sonnet CXLV**

O lips where Love did weave his sweetest thread,
That spake the word which struck my heart with dread—
*“I scorn thee,”* cold as winter’s bitter breath,
While I in silent anguish lay for dead.
Yet when her eyes beheld my pained estate,
A softer light did steal into her gaze,
And pity, like the dawn’s first golden haze,
Did melt the frost of her unkind debate.

*“I scorn,”* she cried, then checked her cruel tongue,
As one who hears a voice from heaven’s height,
And turns from shadows where dark fiends have clung.
So too her heart, once hard as frozen night,
Cast off the word that wrought me bitter wrong,
And breathed, *“Not thee,”*—my soul took wing in light.
