**Sonnet CXIX**

What bitter draughts have steeped my fevered brain,
Drawn from the marsh where love’s false sirens weep,
Where hope and dread in ceaseless war remain,
And triumph fades like shadows in the deep!
What folly’s path hath led my heart astray,
When joy itself was but a fleeting guest,
And eyes, once bright, now dim with tears betray
The wreck of dreams in passion’s wild unrest!

Yet lo! From loss doth wisdom’s light arise,
As ruin’d walls may rise more fair, more high;
And love, once slain, revives with clearer eyes,
More steadfast now beneath the open sky.
 Thus chastened, I to purer bliss return,
 And gain by grief what idle joys would spurn.
