**Sonnet LI**

O laggard steed, why dost thou drag thy pace
When love doth bid me haste unto my dear?
No spur nor whip can match my soul’s embrace,
Nor wind nor wing outstrip my longing here.
What plea shall then thy sluggish limbs defend,
When fire itself seems leaden to my thought?
No beast nor bolt could ever make an end
Of this swift flight where love’s own wings are wrought.
For if my heart could gallop on ahead,
No flesh-bound beast could bear such speed as mine;
Desire, not horse, must draw me where I’m led,
And love itself shall leave thee far behind.
 Then go, dull jade—thy slowness I forgive,
 For I shall run where thou canst never live.
