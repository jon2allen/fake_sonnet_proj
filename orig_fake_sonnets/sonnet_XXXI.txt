**Sonnet XXXI**

O chest where all my lost affections dwell,
Wherein I thought each tender spark had died,
Yet there they breathe, in love’s sweet citadel,
And wake again where once I laid them wide.
How oft my grief in holy tears hath flowed,
As though the dead some tribute still might claim,
Yet lo! they live, in thee their light bestowed,
And all their borrowed grace now bears thy name.
Thou art the vault where buried joys revive,
Adorned with spoils of lovers past and gone,
Who gave their share of me to thee alive,
That now thy single heart holds all their own.
 Their forms I cherished, now in thee I see,
 And thou, their sum, dost hold the whole of me.
