**Sonnet LXXX**

O how my trembling hand doth pause to trace
The lines where once a loftier pen took flight,
Whose verse, like stars, doth gild your name with grace,
While mine, a flicker, fades in borrowed light.
Yet though your worth, a boundless, sunlit sea,
Doth bear the proudest keels with equal cheer,
My frail skiff, humbled by his sovereignty,
Still dares to ride where lesser waves appear.

A breath from you would lift me from the deep,
Whilst he upon the endless tides is borne;
Or should I sink, a wreck the tempests sweep,
He stands unshaken, like a tower sworn.
 Then let him rise while I in shadows stay—
 My love, my fault, my ruin, and my prey.
