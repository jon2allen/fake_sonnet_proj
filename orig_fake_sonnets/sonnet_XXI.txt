**Sonnet XXI**

Not mine the art to gild with borrowed light,
Nor pluck the stars to deck a mortal’s face,
Nor bid the waves their sapphire depths recite,
Nor steal from heaven’s vault its bright embrace.
No painted Muse shall prompt my humble line—
No couplement of proud and hollow cheer,
No April’s bloom nor gems of ocean’s mine
Shall vie with love that needs no gilded gear.

For thou art fair, not as the sun’s fierce ray,
Nor as the moon that pales in borrowed grace,
But as the dawn that parts the night’s delay,
Or as the rose that lifts its modest face.
 Let fools with empty praise their verses swell;
 I sing what’s true, and need not flatter well.
