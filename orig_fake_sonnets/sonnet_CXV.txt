**Sonnet CXV**

O fleeting lines, too bold in youth’s embrace,
That swore my love could never burn more bright—
Yet time, that mocks the proudest human face,
Unfolds new fires from ashes of the night.
What fool would chain the tide with feeble thread,
Or bid the rose in winter keep its hue?
For love, like spring, must ever bow its head
To seasons’ turn, and bloom in forms anew.

Why then did I not dare to crown the hour,
When certainty stood firm on shifting sand?
For love’s not bound by reason’s measured power,
But grows where time and passion bid it stand.
 A child it stays, yet ever climbs the tree—
 So let it rise, though none may foresee.
