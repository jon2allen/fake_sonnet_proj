**Sonnet XXXVII**

As winter’s frost doth clasp the withered vine,
Yet finds in tender bud a fleeting grace,
So I, by time and fate left lame, decline,
Yet in thy radiant face my solace trace.
For where thy virtues bloom—fair form and mind,
Or wit that dances light on fortune’s stage—
There shall my love its firmest roots entwine,
And in thy splendor turn my want to wage.

No more I reckon age nor poverty,
While thy bright shadow lends my soul its hue,
For in thy wealth I stand both rich and free,
And borrow light from all thy glory’s dew.
 Then let me crave what best thy heart may be:
 This boon I gain—blest past all misery.
