**Sonnet XCV**

O fleeting grace that gilds the tarnished frame,
Like gilded ore that hides the rust beneath,
Thy faultless guise doth lend thee borrowed fame,
And wraps thy vice in beauty’s softest breath.
What slanderous tongue, though sharp with envy’s sting,
Could mar thy name when spoken in thy praise?
For even blame must wear a sweeter ring,
And ill itself is lost in thy bright rays.

Yet mark how folly builds its gilded nest
Within thy breast, where every blot is hid
By love’s deceit, that calls the worst the best,
And turns to gold all that thy hands have bid.
Beware, fair soul, this treacherous delight—
The brightest blade grows dull with use unright.
