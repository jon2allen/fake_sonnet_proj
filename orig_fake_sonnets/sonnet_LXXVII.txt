**Sonnet LXXVII**

O mark how Time, that cunning thief, doth steal
The rose from cheek, the fire from lustrous eye,
As glass doth show what youth may not conceal—
The creeping lines where laughter once did lie.
The dial’s hand, with slow and sly deceit,
Doth count the hours that slip like stolen gold,
And leaves thee naught but shadows, cold and fleet,
Where once thy spring in wanton warmth was rolled.

Yet take these leaves, this barren, waiting page,
And bid thy thoughts in silent ink take flight;
For what the mind may lose to fleeting age,
The written word shall guard in endless light.
 So trace thy soul where Time may not erase,
 And find thy youth reborn in written grace.
