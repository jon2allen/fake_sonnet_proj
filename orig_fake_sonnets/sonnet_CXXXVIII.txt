**Sonnet CXXXVIII**

When thou dost vow thy heart is free from art,
I feign to trust thee, though I know thee false,
That thou mayst deem me one untouched by malice,
Unskilled in guile that time and use exalts.
So fondly I pretend thou think’st me green,
Though well I know my spring hath long been spent;
Still I embrace thy honeyed, hollow speech—
Thus truth on both sides lies in silence pent.

Yet why dost thou not own thy treachery?
And why confess I not my silvered years?
O love’s sweet folly is to seem sincere,
And age in love disdains the reckoning clear.
 Then let us clasp our faults in mutual night,
 And in our lies find comfort, brief yet bright.
