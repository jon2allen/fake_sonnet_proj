**Sonnet XC**

Then scorn me when thou must—if scorn thee please,
Now, while the heavens with envious spite conspire,
And fate doth weave my days in tangled ease,
Lest time should dull the edge of thy sharp ire.
Ah, do not wait till fortune’s storm hath passed,
Nor creep behind when sorrow’s tide is spent,
To strike the wound when all my strength is massed,
And leave me twice undone, thy malice lent.
If part thou wilt, then part with haste and flame,
Ere lesser griefs have gnawed my soul to bone,
But in the onset come—so shall I name
Thee first and last of all my woes alone.
 For other pangs, which now seem sharp and dire,
 Compared with thee, are but a fading fire.
