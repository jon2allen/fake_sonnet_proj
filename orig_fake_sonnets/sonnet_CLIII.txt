**Sonnet CLIII**

O fleeting spark by careless Cupid left,
Where Dian’s nymph, with cunning hand and cold,
Did quench the brand in spring’s unyielding deft,
And stole the flame to forge a bath of gold.
Yet what was meant to heal all love’s despair
Now burns with borrowed fire, a fleeting grace—
For though men seek its balm, none find it there,
Save where my lady’s eyes restore the blaze.

For when her glance rekindles Love’s old art,
The boy, with mischief bright, my breast hath tried;
I, fevered, sought the bath to ease my smart,
Yet found no solace where the waters sighed.
 No cure lies here—my wound must ever bleed
 Where Cupid’s torch is lit by her, indeed.
