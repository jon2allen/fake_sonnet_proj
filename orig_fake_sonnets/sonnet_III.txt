**Sonnet III**

Behold thy mirror’s truth, where youth doth gleam,
And mark how fleeting time doth steal thy grace.
For if thou hoard this bloom, a selfish dream,
Thou rob’st the world of beauty’s fair embrace.
What maid so proud would scorn thy tender art,
Or what vain fool would barren keep his bed?
Thou art the glass wherein thy mother’s heart
Doth see her spring in thee anew outspread.
Yet time will mar thy glass with lines of care,
And age shall dim the light that now doth shine.
Then let thy love in another’s form repair,
Lest all thy glory fade in dust’s design.
   But if thou live and leave no heir behind,
   Thy face, thy name, in silent earth confined.
