**Sonnet XLII**

That she is thine doth wound me not so deep,
Yet once my heart did hold her dear above;
But now thy theft doth make my sorrow weep,
A sting more sharp than all the pangs of love.
Yet blame thee not, for love hath made thee blind,
And she, who once was mine, now turns to thee;
For in thy eyes she sees what she did find—
A mirror where my own devotion be.
If thou dost gain, then I must lose my light,
And she, who flees, leaves but an empty name;
Yet in this loss, though both escape my sight,
They bear my cross, and share a borrowed flame.
 But lo! if thou and I are bound as one,
 Then she who clings to thee is still my own.
