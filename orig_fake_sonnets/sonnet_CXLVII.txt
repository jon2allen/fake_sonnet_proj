**Sonnet CXLVII**

O cruel delight, that like a flame doth burn,
Yet feeds the wound it never means to heal;
A thirst unquenched, where reason dares not turn,
Lest truth should break the dream I long to feel.
My wit, once guide, now flees the fevered mind,
Scorned for its counsel, left to rot alone;
And madness rules where wisdom once was kind,
A storm of thoughts in chaos overthrown.

No balm remains, no hope to ease the smart,
For love’s sweet lie hath poisoned all my sight;
I swore thee gold, yet find thee but a dart,
A shadow bright that leads me into night.
Thus blind I grope, where once my heart did see,
And kiss the hand that dooms my misery.
