**Sonnet LV**

Not brass nor towering spires of kingly pride,
Nor sculpted stone where time’s slow hand doth creep,
Shall stand when wars and wasting years deride
The hollow pomp where mortal glories sleep.
Yet in this verse, where fleeting breath takes flight,
Your name shall wake when marble lies undone,
And time’s harsh scythe, that levels all to night,
Shall spare the light my steadfast quill hath spun.
For though the earth may crumble, stars may fade,
And empires sink beneath the tides of years,
This ink, where love and memory are laid,
Shall laugh at ruin, mock at time’s dark fears.
   So live, though dust the flesh must needs confine,
   In lines that outlast death, in love’s design.
