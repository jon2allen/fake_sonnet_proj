**Sonnet XCIV**

They that in virtue stand yet shun the fray,
Who wear no mask nor feign what they are not,
Whose steadfast hearts refuse the world’s decay,
Nor bend to folly though the crowd be wrought—
These are the heirs of time’s unyielding grace,
Who guard the gold of heaven’s bright decree,
And hold their souls in calm, unshaken place,
While others waste their light in vanity.
The rose that blooms in summer’s fleeting span
May charm the air with scent and hue so fair,
Yet if corruption seize its tender plan,
The thorny weed shall mock its glory there.
 For purest things by taint are most undone:
 The lily fouled reeks worse than common sun.
