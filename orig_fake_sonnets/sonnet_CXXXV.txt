**Sonnet CXXXV**

O thou who dost in will’s own name delight,
And bear’st the banners of desire unfurled,
Why dost thou spurn the will that seeks thy sight,
When all the world is but thy willing world?
The stars, though bright, still borrow heaven’s fire,
And rivers, though they run, must kiss the sea;
So let thy will, in boundless grace, aspire
To take my will and make it one with thee.
For what is will but love’s unyielding cry?
And what is love but will in sweet disguise?
Then let not pride nor scorn my suit deny,
Nor turn thy face from these beseeching eyes.
For if thou wilt, then will is all my plea—
And in thy will, my will shall ever be.
