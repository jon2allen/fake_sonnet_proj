**Sonnet CXX**

O cruel fate, that binds our wounded hands,
Where once your scorn did pierce my trembling breast,
Now time hath turned the tide of love’s commands,
And I, who wronged, must plead for mercy’s rest.
For if my heart did once with ice repel,
Then yours, too, knew the sting of cold disdain,
And both our souls in bitter torment dwell,
Till pride and pain have wrought their iron chain.

Yet would that memory, sharp as winter’s breath,
Had taught us both the weight of sorrow’s load,
That we might sooner spare each other’s death,
And heal the wounds our reckless words bestowed.
 But now your fault doth mine in balance weigh,
 And love must pay what love hath torn away.
