**Sonnet LXXIV**

O stay thy grief, for time shall not undo
The bond that binds my verse to thee alone;
When death’s cold hand hath claimed what once was new,
Yet still my words in living light are shown.
For what is flesh but dust to feed the ground?
The soul, thy treasure, soars beyond the grave—
No worm nor knife can mar what love hath crowned,
Nor steal the flame that time cannot deprave.
Then mourn not what the earth must take away,
The husk that held the fire now turned to clay;
But keep this scroll, where love’s own light doth play,
And know my spirit walks in thee alway.
 The worth thou seek’st lies not in mortal frame,
 But in this verse, where love outlives my name.
