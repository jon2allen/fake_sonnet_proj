**Sonnet CXIV**

Or do my senses, drunk on thee alone,
Brew lies like wine to crown a fool’s delight?
Or doth my heart, by some strange art outgrown,
Turn dross to gold in thy unerring light?
What fiend or angel bids my gaze confess
That shadows wear thy face, so fair, so pure?
Doth flattery rule, or love’s unblemished press
Make even faults in thy perfection sure?

Nay, ’tis the first—my sight, beguiled, doth sip
The sweet deceit thy beauty doth impart;
My soul, a king, drinks deep from folly’s cup,
And finds no ill where thou dost play thy part.
 If poison lurks, then let the fault be mine,
 For first my eye, then heart, did thee enshrine.
