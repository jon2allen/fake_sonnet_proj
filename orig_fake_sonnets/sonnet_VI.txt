**Sonnet VI**

O let not time’s ungentle scythe erase
The bloom that lingers yet in thy fair prime,
Nor suffer fleeting hours to mar thy grace
Ere thou hast stored thy sweetness for all time.
For what is beauty but a fleeting spark,
A flame that fades unless it be preserved?
Then kindle thou a light that leaves its mark,
And in thy issue let thy soul be served.

What though the worm shall gnaw thy mortal clay,
If in thy child thy likeness lives anew?
Tenfold thy joy, if tenfold they repay
The love thou gav’st, and prove thy virtues true.
Then yield not to the grave’s unyielding claim,
But cheat decay, and live in deathless fame.
