**Sonnet CXLIII**

Behold, as yonder hind doth flee the hound,
Whose panting breath doth scorch the trodden ground,
So dost thou fly, yet turn thy gaze not round,
Nor mark the tears that stain my weary ground.
The chase is long, the quarry swift and sly,
Yet still I follow, though my strength be spent,
As one who seeks the light ‘neath stormy sky,
Or moth that burns in flame of vain intent.
But shouldst thou pause, and let thy heart relent,
And bid thy love to stay its restless flight,
Then shall my soul in joyful homage bent
Kneel at thy feet, and bless thee day and night.
 So grant me grace, or leave me to despair,
 For love unmet is death beyond compare.
