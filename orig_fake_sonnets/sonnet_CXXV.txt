**Sonnet CXXV**

What boots it me to bear the gilded weight,
Or raise fair towers that time shall soon erase,
When all the pomp of fortune’s fleeting state
Proves but a breath, a shadow, or a haze?
Have I not seen the vain in folly’s school,
Who barter peace for gaudy, borrowed show,
And, like the moth, in flame of folly’s rule,
Consume their souls for what they cannot know?
Nay, let me dwell where love is pure and true,
Unstained by art, untouched by pride’s alloy,
Where no false coin nor counterfeit will do,
But hearts in honest trade their wealth employ.
 Then, false accuser, hence! The soul stands clear,
 When least thy tainted breath can wound it near.
