**Sonnet XII**

When I behold the glass where moments flee,
And mark how golden noon is drowned in shade,
When once-lush blooms now wither, pale to see,
And youth’s bright fire in ashen locks betrayed;
When naked boughs, that once did shade the mead,
Now rattle bare beneath the winter’s breath,
And fields once green lie bound in sheaves to feed
The hungry maw of time, that spares not death—
Then do I turn mine eyes to thee, and fear
That thou, too, must be reaped by time’s cold hand,
For beauty fades, and love itself grows sere,
As all things bow to his unyielding brand.
 Yet in thy grace some seed may yet take root,
 To bloom anew when thou art turned to dust.
