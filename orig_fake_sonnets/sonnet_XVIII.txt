**Sonnet XVIII**

O might I liken thee to dawn’s first light,
Whose gentle beams outshine the fleeting sun?
No storm may mar thy grace, nor blight thy might,
Nor time’s unkind hand steal what thou hast won.
The rose may fade, the lily lose its hue,
And golden hours in shadow slip away,
Yet thou dost keep thy splendor ever true,
Unbowed by fate or fortune’s fickle sway.
No winter’s grasp shall chill thy radiant glow,
Nor death’s cold touch thy living fire subdue,
For in these lines thy praise shall ever flow,
And time itself shall yield its debt to you.
   So while one heart may beat, or eye may see,
   So long endures this verse, and thou in me.
