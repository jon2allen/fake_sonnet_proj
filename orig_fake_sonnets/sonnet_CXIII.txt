**Sonnet CXIII**

Since thou didst turn thy face from mine own gaze,
My soul doth walk in shadows, lost, undone,
As though the sun had fled the noonday blaze,
And left me wandering ‘neath a moonless sun.
No bloom that nods upon the dewy mead,
No lark that soars in skies of sapphire bright,
No tempest’s roar, no whisper of the reed,
Can wake my heart from this thy ghostly light.

For all I see is but a borrowed hue,
A fleeting shape that fades to thine own grace,
As though the world were but a mirror true,
Reflecting still thy form, thy sweet embrace.
Thus bound in chains of love’s unyielding art,
My sight is blind, for thou dost rule my heart.
