**Sonnet XXIV**

O artless hand that limns with borrowed light,
Thy love’s fair form in chambers of my mind,
Where time nor touch may mar the work refin’d,
Nor fleeting hours the hues of grace recite.
Yet lo! the glass through which thy soul takes flight
Is but a lens by fancy’s craft design’d—
A mirror dim where truth is half confin’d,
And love’s deep pulse beats hidden from the sight.

For though my gaze hath traced each line so true,
And thine eyes’ glow doth lend my verse its fire,
No brush nor pen may paint the heart entire.
What shadows dance where passion’s currents strew?
 The face may lie, the lips may feign desire,
 But love’s pure flame no mortal art may woo.
