**Sonnet CIX**

O! cast not on my faith a shadow’s doubt,
Though time and tide may stretch our bond asunder,
No force nor fate could tear my soul throughout,
For in thy breast my truest self doth wander.
Thou art the haven where my heart doth rest,
And if I stray, like pilgrim lost in night,
I turn again, by love’s own compass pressed,
Unchanged in will, though altered in my sight.
Yet shouldst thou fear the frailties of my kind,
The fleeting storms that shake this mortal frame,
Know that no shadow could my soul unbind,
Nor dim the light thy constancy doth claim.
 For all the world is but a hollow shell,
 Save thee, my star, in whom my heavens dwell.
