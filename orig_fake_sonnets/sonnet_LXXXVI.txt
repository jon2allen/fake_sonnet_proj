**Sonnet LXXXVI**

Did some bright rival’s quill, with art so keen,
Steal forth the fire that lit my trembling line?
Or did his verse, like Phoebus’ noonday sheen,
Outshine my muse and leave my thoughts as twine?
Nay, not his hand, nor spirits that inspire,
Could quench the spark wherewith my soul was fraught;
No ghostly guide, nor night’s deceitful choir,
Could still the voice that love itself had taught.
Yet when your face did grace his stately lay,
And in his lines your beauty found its throne,
Then did my words, like shadows, fade away,
For all my strength was yours, and yours alone.
   So let him boast—his verse may rise or fall—
   My silence speaks what words can ne’er recall.
