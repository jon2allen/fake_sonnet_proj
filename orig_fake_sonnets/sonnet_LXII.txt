**Sonnet LXII**

O pride, thou viper coiled in flesh and bone,
That gnaws my breast and feeds on flatteries sweet,
No balm may soothe, no physic may atone
For thy deep wound where self-regard doth meet.
Mine eyes, once clear, now see but mine own face,
Mine ears but echo mine own praise alone,
And in this glass of vain and fleeting grace,
I crown myself with honors not mine own.

Yet when the light of truth doth pierce the shade,
And time’s harsh hand hath marred what once was fair,
I shrink to see the folly I have made—
A king of dust, a god of empty air.
Then turn I, humbled, to thy brighter ray,
And borrow light to gild my dying day.
