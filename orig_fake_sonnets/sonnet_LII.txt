**Sonnet LII**

O am I as the miser, hoarding gold,
Whose hidden wealth lies sealed in vaulted night,
Too dear to waste on each unthinking sight,
Lest common touch should dull its burnished hold.
Thus feasts are rare, and jewels, bright and cold,
Are set but sparingly in crowns of light,
Lest frequent gaze should steal their borrowed might—
So time doth guard thee, treasure to behold.

Yet not as dust in coffers dost thou rest,
Nor sleep in folds of silk untouched, unseen,
But wake to grace the hour thou mak’st most blest,
When thy fair form doth break its prison’s sheen.
 Blest art thou, whose worth doth bid me rise,
 To hold thee fast, or else in longing sigh.
