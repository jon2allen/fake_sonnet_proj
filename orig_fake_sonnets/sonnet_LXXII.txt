**Sonnet LXXII**

O let not time demand what I have been,
Nor seek in dust the echoes of my name,
For love, once bright, now fades to ghostly gleam,
And all my worth is but a borrowed flame.
If you should feign what never was in me,
And gild my faults with praise too rich, too kind,
Then truth shall mock what mercy would decree,
And love itself grow false in mercy’s mind.

No, let the earth reclaim what it has claimed,
Let silence wrap the bones that once were mine,
Nor let your heart be by my shadow shamed,
Nor let my failings dim your light divine.
 For what I was could never match your grace,
 And love deserves no less than love’s true face.
