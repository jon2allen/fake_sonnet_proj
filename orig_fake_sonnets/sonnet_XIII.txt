**Sonnet XIII**

O! would that time might pause, and you remain,
Yet fleeting life denies what love would keep—
The rose must fade, the golden sun must wane,
And mortal hands no earthly joys may reap.
Then take this glass wherein your self is shown,
And bid it live when you to dust descend,
That beauty’s lease, though brief, may still be known,
And in your child your vanished grace extend.
What fool would let such splendour turn to dust,
When wisdom’s hand might bid it bloom anew?
Why yield to death what love and care might just
Preserve in flesh as fair as heaven’s hue?
 O! heedless heart, if you would cheat the grave,
 Let not your light in silent darkness wave.
