**Sonnet LVIII**

O let me kneel, yet never lift mine eye
To question where your fleeting hours do roam,
Nor dare to count the moments that deny
My bounden heart its solace or its home.
What though my soul in silent durance pines,
A captive chained to fortune’s fickle wheel?
Still must I bear the weight of love’s designs,
Nor murmur once, though fate my patience steal.
Go where you will—your sovereignty is sure,
No law may bid your liberty obey,
Nor I, though wrung with sorrow most obscure,
Presume to chide the path you choose to stray.
 Then wait I must, though waiting prove my bane,
 And love your will, though it should rend in twain.
