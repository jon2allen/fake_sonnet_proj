**Sonnet LXXXVIII**

When time shall bid thee weigh my worth as naught,
And cast my faith to winds of scornful spite,
Then shall my tongue, by love’s own fire taught,
Defend thy name though thou dost me despite.
For well I know the faults that lurk within,
The hidden stains no outward eye may see,
Yet still I’ll paint them bright in thy renown,
That thou, in casting me, may’st crowned be.

And I, though bruised, shall find my gain therein,
For all my thoughts to thee are bent so true,
That every wound I take for thee shall win
A double grace—both thine and mine anew.
 Such is the bond that chains my heart to thee,
 I’ll bear all wrongs if thou but blameless be.
