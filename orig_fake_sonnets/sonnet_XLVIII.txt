**Sonnet XLVIII**

O how I wrought with wary hand to keep
Each fleeting grace in caskets sealed with care,
Lest time or treachery should make them cheap,
Or common hands profane what once was rare.
Yet thou, my soul’s bright jewel, my only light,
Whom locks nor bars could ever hope to bind,
Now stand’st exposed to every envious sight,
A spoil for folly, left to fate unkind.

No vault nor coffer guards thee from the throng,
Nor iron key thy sacred worth confines;
Thou dwell’st within my breast, yet not for long—
For even there, where love most safe reclines,
Thou shalt be ravished, stolen, borne away,
By thievish time, who covets such as thee.
