**Sonnet LX**

As rivers race to kiss the waiting sand,
So fleeting hours dissolve in time’s embrace;
Each moment yields to one yet unplanned,
And all press on in swift, relentless chase.
The dawn that lifts the babe from shadow’s keep
Soon climbs to noon, then bows to evening’s claim,
While fate, with crooked hand, doth mar and sweep
The gifts once bright with fortune’s fleeting flame.
Time carves his name in youth’s unlined face,
And reaps the bloom that nature deigns to sow,
Yet still my verse defies his dark disgrace,
And stands where all else withers, fades, and goes.
 So let the scythe descend, the clock unwind—
 Thy praise shall live when time itself is blind.
