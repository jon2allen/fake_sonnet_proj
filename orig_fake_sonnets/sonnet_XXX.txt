**Sonnet XXX**

When shadows stretch and dusk begins to creep,
I call to mind the joys that time hath reft,
And sigh for dreams that sleep in slumber deep,
While grief revives what sorrow long hath left.
Then do mine eyes, unskilled in sorrow’s art,
Pour forth their tribute to the silent dead,
And love’s old wounds reopen, torn apart,
As ghosts of bliss through memory’s halls are led.
Yet more I mourn the sorrows left unsaid,
And count again each loss in weary sum,
As though the debt were never fully paid,
And time itself had mocked my heart’s dull hum.
 But when thy face doth rise within my mind,
 All past regrets in thy sweet light unwind.
