**Sonnet CVIII**

O tell me, quill, what hidden thought doth dwell
Within this vault where love’s sweet fire doth burn,
That hath not yet in trembling lines been shown,
Nor shaped the praise my raptured heart must tell?
What phrase untried, what unturned metaphor,
Might catch the light that in thy beauty glows?
Naught new remains—yet still my verse outpours
The selfsame hymn my constant soul bestows.

For time, that gnaws the rose with ruthless tooth,
Shall never mar the love thy grace hath wrought;
Nor wrinkled age, nor fortune’s shifting ruth,
Can dim the flame where first thy light was caught.
So in my breast, though years may turn to dust,
Love lives, defiant of the grave’s cold thrust.
