**Sonnet CVI**

When through the scrolls of ages long outworn,
I mark the shapes of grace in faded line,
Where poets wove in verse both bright and torn
The praise of forms now lost to dust and time,
Then in their art—though quaint, though frail, though old—
I trace the curve of cheek, the fire of gaze,
And know their hands, though bound by chains of gold,
Would fain have limned the light of these our days.

For all their songs were but the shadows cast
Of beauty yet to bloom in later years,
And though their sight was dimmed by distance vast,
They saw enough to stir both love and tears.
 Yet we, who walk where time’s full splendor lies,
 Stand mute before such glory—wanting skies.
