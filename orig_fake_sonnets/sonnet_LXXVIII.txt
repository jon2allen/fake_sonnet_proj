**Sonnet LXXVIII**

Oft have I called upon thy sacred name,
Whose breath doth lend my trembling lines their fire,
As every hand that steals thy borrowed flame
Doth spread thy praise in verse both bold and dire.
Thine eyes, bright stars that guide the lost to light,
Have taught the mute to sing, the blind to see,
And gilded wisdom’s wing with purer flight,
While grace doth borrow grace to walk with thee.
Yet boast thou most in this my humble lay,
Whose soul is thine, whose every thought thy own,
For others’ works but borrow what thou’st sown,
And deck their lines with graces thou dost pay.
 Thou art my art, my guide, my only skill,
 And lift’st my want to heights I never will.
