**Sonnet XXVIII**

How shall my weary soul find rest’s release,
When day and night conspire to steal my peace?
The sun, with golden chains, denies me ease,
While night, in sable robes, prolongs my grief.
Each, sworn foes to the other’s fleeting reign,
Yet join in cruel league to wound my breast—
The day with labor’s weight, the night with pain,
Both mock the love that keeps me dispossessed.

I sing the sun’s bright praise to cheer his light,
And call thee fair when clouds his glory mar;
So too I flatter night’s unyielding might,
When stars grow dim and thou dost shine afar.
 Yet day by day my sorrow’s thread is spun,
 And night by night my anguish weighs as one.
