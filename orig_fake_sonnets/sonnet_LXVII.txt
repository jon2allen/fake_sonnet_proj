**Sonnet LXVII**

Why doth the world still cling to tarnished gold,
And crown the thief who steals the light away?
Why praise the gilded lie, so false, so cold,
When truth stands bare in rags of yesterday?
O, why should art with borrowed hues contrive
To mimic life where life itself decays?
Why chase the ghost of beauty, fugitive,
When living grace in purest form still stays?

Now Nature, spent, doth beg from Time’s own hand,
Her coffers drained, her treasures all undone.
Yet still she hoards one jewel, bright and grand—
A spark to prove what splendors she had won.
 So let him live, though all else fade and fall,
 A last true light to shame the counterfeit’s pall.
