**Sonnet CX**

O weary heart, that strayed in folly’s maze,
And wore the fool’s bright coat for all to see,
Who bartered gold for dross in wanton days,
And turned fresh wounds to scars most bitterly.
Yet in that wandering, though I shunned the light,
And bent my gaze from truth’s unflinching eye,
Those very faults did prove my soul’s true might,
And taught me love where lesser men would fly.
Now let the past be past, and all things cease—
No more shall doubt my steadfast faith assail,
Nor time nor trial shake my deep-set peace,
For thou art fixed, my star, my holy grail.
 Then take me in, where love and mercy blend,
 And be my rest, my end, my beginning’s end.
