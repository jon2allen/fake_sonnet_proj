**Sonnet XIX**

O cruel Time, that gnaws the mountain’s height,
And wears the sea to naught with ceaseless flow,
That dims the star’s unblinking, steadfast light,
And steals the rose’s blush ere noon may know;
Thou turn’st the king to dust, the oak to clay,
And mak’st the child’s bright laugh a withered sigh;
Yet scorn not thou to spare what I would stay—
The grace that in my love’s fair face doth lie.

Let not thy scythe the golden tresses shear,
Nor let thy frost upon her brow descend;
Preserve the fire that doth her eyes endear,
Lest beauty’s lamp in time’s dark night should end.
 Yet mark me well, though thou dost all undo,
 My verse shall keep her ever bright and new.
