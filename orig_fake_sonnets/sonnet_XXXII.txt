**Sonnet XXXII**

When time hath sealed my weary eyes in clay,
And thou dost walk where once my footsteps trod,
Perchance thou’lt chance upon this artless lay,
Scrawled by the hand that loved thee, now a sod.

Then weigh it not ‘gainst fashion’s gilded line,
Nor scorn its faults where polish’d wits excel;
For though the world may deem my verse divine,
‘Twas writ in love, not cunning’s hollow shell.

Yet grant me this, when thou dost turn the page:
“Had fate but spared him to this brighter day,
His muse had soared where now the wise engage,
And matched the best in skill’s unerring way.

 So let them keep their laurels, bright and high—
 I’ll read their art, but hold his love most nigh.”
