**Sonnet XLIII**

When dusk descends and steals the sun’s bright gaze,
Mine eyes, by day so dull with common sight,
At night awake to tread thy shadowed ways,
And in the dark find thee in borrowed light.
O fleeting ghost that haunts the dreaming mind,
How might thy form in waking splendor gleam,
When even now thy shade leaves sight refined,
And turns the blackest void to radiant beam?
Would not my soul in daylight’s truth be blessed,
To see thee whole, not half in slumber’s art,
When night’s cold hand upon my lids is pressed,
And thou dost dwell but in a wandering part?
 Yet day is night till thou dost stand revealed,
 And night is dawn when dreams thy face have sealed.
