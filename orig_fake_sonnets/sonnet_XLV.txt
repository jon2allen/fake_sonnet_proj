**Sonnet XLV**

O fleeting breath and flame that burn so bright,
Yet dwell not here but in thy distant sphere,
The one my fleeting dream, the other light,
Both swift as shadows when the sun draws near.

For when these messengers of love depart,
To bear my sighs and longing to thine ear,
My soul, once whole, now torn by cruel art,
Doth waste in grief, its very life grown sere.

Till swift they fly, returning with thy grace,
And whisper news of thee to ease my pain,
Yet scarce their tidings warm this frozen place
Ere I must send them forth to thee again.

Thus joy and sorrow in one moment blend,
And love’s sweet torment knows no perfect end.
