**Sonnet XI**

O fleeting form, that dost in time unfold,
Like morning’s bloom that fades ere noon is high,
Yet in thy fading, seeds of gold are sown—
New life from thee shall rise when thou dost die.
For wisdom lies not in the hoard of years,
But in the gift bestowed on time’s swift stream;
Nor beauty fades where love the soul endears,
But blooms anew in youth’s unspent dream.
Let barren hearts, by nature’s hand unblest,
Wither unseen where no kind branch may cling;
But thou, whom heaven hath crowned with richest grace,
Must scatter wide the treasure thou dost bring.
 For thou wert shaped to be the world’s increase,
 Not shrink to naught, but live in endless peace.
