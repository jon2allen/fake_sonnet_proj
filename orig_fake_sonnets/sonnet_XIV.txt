**Sonnet XIV**

Not from the heavens do I seek my lore,
Nor mark the wandering stars’ unsteady light,
Nor prophesy of storms or harvest’s store,
Nor read in fate what princes may requite.
No whispered augury from spheres above
Can tell the hour’s brief joy or sorrow’s sting,
Nor fix the crown’s fall or the heart’s true love—
Such things are writ where mortal pulses cling.

But in thine eyes, a steadfast heaven I see,
Where truth and beauty burn in one clear flame,
And there I learn what time may yet decree,
If thou wouldst keep what thou dost now proclaim.
 Yet if thou turn from this celestial art,
 Thy doom shall be truth’s death and beauty’s part.
