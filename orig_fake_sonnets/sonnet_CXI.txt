**Sonnet CXI**

O! chide not Fortune for her cruel decree,
Nor blame the stars that weave my wretched fate,
For lo, my soul is bound in misery,
And time hath marked me with a brand of late.
The world’s harsh gaze hath stained my purest part,
As dyer’s hand doth seep into the cloth,
And now my heart, once light, is dark with art,
A prisoner to the ills that time hath wrought.

Yet mercy’s hand may lift this weight from me,
If thou wilt but a moment’s pity lend;
I’ll drink the draught, though bitter it may be,
And bear the sting to make my sorrows end.
Then turn thy gaze, let kindness be my guide,
And in thy grace, my wounded soul abide.
