**Sonnet CXXXI**

O cruelest star that rules my night’s despair,
Whose light doth mock the shadows thou dost cast,
Thy beauty’s flame burns brightest when most rare,
Yet leaves my soul in ashes, scorched and vast.
They say thy glance could melt the winter’s frost,
That none dare gaze upon thy face and live—
But I, who know thee best, am doubly lost,
For love and grief thy bitter gifts do give.

Yet mark me well: though others call thee cold,
And whisper slander of thy heart’s deceit,
I swear by heaven’s vault and stars untold,
No frost nor fire could make thy love more sweet.
 In form thou art all grace, in act unkind,
 And thus the world misjudges what I find.
