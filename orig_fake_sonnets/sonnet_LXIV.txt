**Sonnet LXIV**

When I behold how Time, with ruthless sway,
Unthreads the pomp of kings in dusty tombs,
And towers that kissed the sky now waste away,
Their stones outworn by wind and weeping glooms;
When I perceive the sea’s insatiate tide
Encroach upon the lands where cities stood,
And fields once green in salted ruin hide,
While waves devour what once was firm and good;
When I mark how all things shift and fade—
The crown, the scepter, love’s most tender vow—
Then grief doth teach my trembling heart to dread
That Time shall steal thee from me, even now.
This thought doth strike such terror through my breast,
I cling to thee as to a fleeting guest.
