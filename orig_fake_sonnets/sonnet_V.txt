**Sonnet V**

O fleeting grace, that time did first bestow,
Whose tender touch did shape each perfect line,
Now turns his hand to mar what once did shine,
And steals the bloom where youthful roses grow.
For lo, the sun that warmed the summer’s glow
Now yields to frost, and all fair things decline;
The sap doth freeze, the boughs no leaves entwine,
And beauty fades beneath the winter’s snow.

Yet were no art to stay the hand of years,
No balm to keep what time would steal away,
Then memory itself would melt in tears,
And naught remain but ashes cold as clay.
But pressed in glass, the flower’s sweet soul stays,
Though form be lost, its essence ne’er decays.
