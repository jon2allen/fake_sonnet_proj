**Sonnet LXXXIV**

Who dares to speak, yet speaks not half so true
As this poor praise: that thou art only thee,
Whose boundless grace no equal shape may shew,
Nor rival form in nature’s realm be free?
O barren quill, that lends no borrowed light,
Nor gilds thy worth with faint and feeble art!
Yet he who writes, if he but set aright
The mirror true, doth honor well his part.
Let him but trace what in thy self is shown,
Nor mar with clumsy hand what heaven designed,
And such a glass shall make his verse well-known,
Where thy fair truth in purest lines is signed.
 Yet thou, in seeking more, dost but impair
 The praise that else had stood beyond compare.
