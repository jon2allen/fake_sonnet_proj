**Sonnet LXXI**

When time hath sealed my lips with earth’s cold hand,
And worms have woven silence through my bones,
Let not thy grief outlive what fate commands,
Nor wake the echoes of my vanished tones.
For if thou read these lines when I am dust,
Forget the quill that shaped each trembling word—
I’d rather rest in some forgotten crust
Than see thy heart by sorrow’s weight deterred.

O, shouldst thou chance upon this fading scroll,
When clay hath claimed what once was warm and bright,
Speak not my name, nor let thy sorrow roll—
But let thy love dissolve with fading light.
Lest time, that laughs at all our fleeting breath,
Should turn thy mourning to a jest in death.
