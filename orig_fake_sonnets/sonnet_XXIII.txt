**Sonnet XXIII**

Like one who stands before a kingly throne,
Yet knows not how to shape his humble plea,
Or like the lute that strains to make its moan,
But snaps its strings beneath too fierce a key—
So I, by love’s own weight o’erwhelmed, stand dumb,
And dare not speak the vows my heart would frame,
Lest words, too weak, should break the sacred sum,
Or faltering tongue profane love’s holy name.
Then let my gaze, more true than spoken art,
Bear witness to the fire my lips conceal,
And let my eyes, with silent eloquence, impart
What trembling speech could never well reveal.
 O! read the book where love’s pure script is penned—
 To see with heart is love’s most perfect end.
