**Sonnet LVI**

O steadfast flame, rekindle yet thy might,
Lest time’s dull hand should blunt thy piercing ray,
For though to-day thou feast upon delight,
To-morrow’s thirst must bid thee burn anew.
So fill thine eyes with love’s unmeasured store,
Yet leave them hungry for the morrow’s sight,
Lest satiety should chain thee evermore,
And quench the fire that burns with purest light.

Let absence be the tide that parts the shore,
Where two true hearts in patient hope abide,
That when at last they meet as once before,
Their joy may shine with treble-laden pride.
Or call it frost, whose bitter, fleeting reign
Makes summer’s kiss more sweet, more rare, more fain.
