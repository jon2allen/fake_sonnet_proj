**Sonnet CI**

O wayward quill, what penance must thou pay
For truth’s bright hue in beauty’s shade undone?
For both in her do live, and there display
Their purest light—yet thou hast left unsung.
Speak, idle tool: wilt thou not thus reply,
“Truth wears no mask, nor needs the painter’s art,
For beauty’s self is truth’s own native dye,
And purest gold wants not the gilded part”?
Yet silence serves not, for thy charge is clear—
To raise her name where time’s slow hand would mar,
To make her grace through endless years appear,
And bid the stars themselves her worth declare.
 Then do thy task, my Muse; let time agree
 To keep her bright as now she shines in thee.
