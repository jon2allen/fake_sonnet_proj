**Sonnet LIV**

O! What fair grace doth gild the fleeting hour,
When truth and beauty in one frame unite,
As dawn’s first blush doth borrow heaven’s power,
And goldens all the world with borrowed light.
The lily stands in pride, yet pale and cold,
Till summer’s kiss doth wake her hidden flame;
So too the heart, though fair in form and mould,
Wanteth the fire that makes a soul its claim.
The painted mask may charm the careless eye,
Yet fades like dew when noon’s harsh breath is near;
But love, once tried by time’s unyielding sky,
Burns all the brighter for the trial clear.
 Then, when thy spring shall yield to winter’s reign,
 My verse shall keep thy truth alive again.
