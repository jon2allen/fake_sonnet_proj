**Sonnet LXXIX**

When first my trembling hand did thee invoke,
Thy name alone did gild my barren line;
No borrowed light, no artifice of smoke,
But truth unfeigned, and passion half-divine.
Yet now, alas, my faltering verse grows cold,
And weaker strains usurp thy rightful throne—
As though some thief had stol’n thy grace of old,
And left me nought but echoes of mine own.

O, let them praise thee with their borrowed fire,
And deck thy brow with flowers not thine own!
They rob thy cheek to lend thee false desire,
And steal thy virtues but to make them shown.
 Yet take no thanks, for what they dare impart
 Is but the debt they owe thy perfect heart.
