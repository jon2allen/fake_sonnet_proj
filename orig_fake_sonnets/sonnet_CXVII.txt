**Sonnet CXVII**

O charge me not with cold and careless heart,
That left your worth in shadowed neglect,
Nor call me false who played the faithless part,
When time and tide your steadfast love reject.
I roamed where stranger eyes did bid me stay,
And squandered hours your patience might have claimed,
Set sail where winds of folly blew astray,
And left your shore for shores untamed, unnamed.

Yet write my faults in ledgers dark and deep,
Heap surmise high where proof may never rise;
Let scorn like winter’s frost my soul outstrip,
But spare the bolt your wrath would sacrifice.
 For all my straying, this I dare avow—
 To test the gold, I struck the fire somehow.
