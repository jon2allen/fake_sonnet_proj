**Sonnet XV**

When I behold how fleeting all things be,
How brief their bloom ere Time doth steal their grace,
How this vast world’s but shadows we do see,
And heaven’s cold eye doth mark each mortal place—
Then mark I how like herbs we rise and fall,
Now bold with sap, now withered by the year,
How pride must fade, how fame must heed time’s call,
And all our glory vanish, dark and drear.
Yet in this dance of mutability,
Thy youth doth shine as fixed as morning’s light,
Though wasting hours conspire with cruelty
To dim thy day and plunge thee into night.
 Thus war I with the thief that would undo,
 And in my verse thy spring I still renew.
