**Sonnet CXXXI**

Her voice is not the lark’s clear morning cry,
Nor doth her touch the trembling lute’s sweet art;
No golden beams from heaven light her eye,
Nor pearls outshine the teeth that part her heart.
The violet’s hue is not so deep as hers,
Nor does the rose in crimson pride outvie
The blush that steals where passion’s fire stirs—
Yet scorn not love that dares not feign a lie.

Her step is not the swan’s upon the stream,
Nor doth her hand the fragrant myrtle bear;
No angel’s grace in mortal guise doth gleam,
Yet who would trade such truth for fleeting air?
For though she walks in shadows, plain and true,
No painted saint could ever match my view.
