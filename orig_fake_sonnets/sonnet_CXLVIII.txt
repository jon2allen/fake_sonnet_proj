**Sonnet CXLVIII**

O cruel! what glass doth Love in darkness frame,
That shows me shapes where no true form abides?
Or if my sight some semblance yet proclaims,
Why doth my heart in falsehood’s maze abide?
If beauty’s ghost my wandering eyes beguile,
Why scorns the world my folly’s painted dream?
If none there be, then Love doth mock the while,
And proves his art more false than Fancy’s scheme.
How can his eye, by passion’s storm oppressed,
Judge fairly when his tears the view confound?
No wonder then if I, by shadows pressed,
Mistake the dawn for twilight’s hollow sound.
 O wily Love! with mist thou dost me bind,
 Lest truth unmask thee, and thy frauds unwind.
