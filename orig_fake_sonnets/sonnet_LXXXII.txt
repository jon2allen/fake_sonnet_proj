**Sonnet LXXXII**

O, do not bind thy soul to one poor pen,
Nor chain thy light where lesser lamps may burn;
Let others gild thee with their borrowed art,
Yet know their praise but shadows thee in turn.
Thou art a flame no single verse can hold,
A star that mocks the reach of mortal song;
Then seek the tongues that time shall yet unfold,
And let their fresher notes thy worth prolong.

Yet when they weave their webs of cunning speech,
With honeyed words that drip like summer’s gold,
Remember still the heart that dares to teach
The truth in lines unvarnished, plain, and bold.
For painted lips may suit a pallid face,
But thee they mar—thy fire needs no such grace.
