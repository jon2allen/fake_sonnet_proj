**Sonnet XCII**

Yet try thy flight, though fate hath bound thee fast,
For time nor tide shall loose what love hath tied;
No breath of life shall linger when thou’st passed,
For thou art life, and life in thee doth bide.
Then fear I not the shadows thou may’st cast,
Nor storms that rise when thou dost turn aside.
A brighter crown than thine own will I grasp,
Where no false dawn may mock my steadfast pride.

Thou canst not shake me with thy fleeting art,
Since all my joy in thy true heart doth rest.
O blest estate! To hold thee, hold my heart,
And blest to lose thee, if such loss be best.
 Yet what is pure that time may not deface?
 Thou may’st deceive, yet never know my grace.
