**Sonnet CIII**

O cruel lack! My pen doth falter so,
When beauty’s self doth bid my verse arise,
Yet all my toil but dims the radiant glow
That in thy face doth mock my feeble guise.
What need hath truth of gilded words to shine?
Thy mirror holds a light no art may frame,
And every line I write but steals the fine
Pure lustre that thy native grace doth claim.
Then let me cease, nor mar with labored art
What heaven hath wrought in perfect, flawless hue—
For what are words but shadows of thy heart,
Or ink but stains where living light shines through?
 Yet if my love must speak, then let it be
 Less than thy worth, and more, dear love, than me.
