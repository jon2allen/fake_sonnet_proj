**Sonnet XCVI**

Some call thy stain a fleeting, wayward flame,
Some praise the light that dances in thine eye;
Yet both the bright and blemished bear thy name,
And faults in thee to virtues magnify.
As gilded crowns ennoble common stone,
So flaws in thee seem virtues, rich and rare,
And eyes that gaze on thee are swiftly won,
Deceived by grace none other can compare.
What hearts might bend if thou shouldst play the thief,
And steal with looks what words could ne’er obtain!
What souls might stray, beguiled by thy brief
And borrowed charms, if thou shouldst feign in vain?
 Yet hold thy peace, nor lend thy voice to guile—
 For mine thou art, and mine thy fairest style.
