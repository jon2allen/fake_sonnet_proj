**Sonnet XXXIII**

O fleeting dawn that crown’st the hill with gold,
And bath’st the fields in amber’s tender grace,
Whose touch doth turn the silver brook to bright,
Till envious mists thy radiant face deface—
So too my star, with beams of love untold,
Did gild my days in one transcendent hour,
Till fortune’s hand, with malice overbold,
Veiled his light in sorrow’s shadowed bower.
Yet though the heavens their borrowed glory stain,
And earthly suns must fade in time’s despite,
No storm shall dim the love I still retain,
Nor clouds obscure the faith that burns so bright.
 For though the world may darken, brief its reign—
 True constancy outshines the sun’s disdain.
