**Sonnet XCI**

Some chase the fleeting praise of noble line,
Some in their cunning hands or wit take pride,
Some hoard the gold that gleams like sunlit wine,
Some trust the strength their mortal limbs abide.
Some deck their frames in silk of foreign dye,
Some hunt with hawks or ride the fleeting steed,
Yet all these toys of time I pass them by—
One treasure doth my weary soul exceed.
For love outshines the blazon of a name,
Outvalues wealth, outlasts the fading thread,
More sweet than falcon’s flight or horse’s flame,
And in thy grace my every boast is bred.
 Yet here my fear doth gnaw my heart with dread:
 That thou mayst leave me, and I be but dead.
