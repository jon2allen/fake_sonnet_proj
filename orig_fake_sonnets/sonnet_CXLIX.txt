**Sonnet CXLIX**

O faithless light! Dost thou deny my flame,
When all my days in thy bright shadow burn?
What though I scorn the selfsame praise and blame,
Yet still thy frown doth make my spirit turn.
Who calls thee foe that I do hold most dear?
Whose scorn I court with sighs and endless woe?
Nay, when thou fleest, I chase thee—none so near—
And wound myself with darts thou dost not throw.
What pride is mine that dares thy scorn defy,
When every breath I draw is but thy will?
My soul, a slave, doth to thy faults comply,
And finds in chains the sweetest torment still.
 Then hate, sweet hate, for now I know thy art:
 Thou lov’st the wise, but I am blind—thy heart.
