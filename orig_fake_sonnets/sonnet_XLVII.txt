**Sonnet XLVII**

O pact ‘twixt sight and soul in sweet accord,
Where each doth lend the other timely grace—
The eye, when starved, doth feed on love’s record,
The heart, when pined, doth seek thy shadowed face.
Then gaze I on thy likeness, fair and bright,
And bid my heart partake the painted cheer;
Or else my heart doth draw mine eye to light,
And there in dreams thy presence doth appear.
So, whether by thy form or love’s own art,
Thou dost not leave me, though thou be not near;
No farther than my thoughts mayst thou depart,
For there I hold thee, and thou hold’st me dear.
 Yet if my thoughts should slumber, dull with woe,
 Thy image wakes them to my joy’s bestow.
