**Sonnet XLIX**

Against that hour when time shall turn his glass,
And thou, once kind, dost weigh my faults with scorn,
When love’s bright flame, now quenched, shall coldly pass,
And reason’s scale thy former grace outworn;
Against that day when thou shalt turn from me,
And in thy glance no spark of warmth remain,
When time hath wrought what time alone can see—
A heart once bound, now loosed by just disdain;
Against that fate I fortify my breast,
With knowledge deep of what my worth may be,
And raise this hand to plead my own unrest,
Lest thou shouldst find just cause to set me free.
 For thou hast law to bid me love adieu,
 Since love must rest on grounds I never knew.
