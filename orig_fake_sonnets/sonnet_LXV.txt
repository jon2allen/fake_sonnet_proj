**Sonnet LXV**

What iron wall, what adamantine tower,
What fortress built of unrelenting stone,
May hope to stand ‘gainst Time’s unyielding power,
Or guard the bloom that fleeting hours have sown?
Lo! where the rose, once proud in crimson dress,
Now withers ‘neath the frost’s unkindly hand,
And marble tombs, though carved with loveliest grace,
Must yield their dead to dust’s unfeeling sand.

O cruel decree! What vault so deep, what art
May shield the gem that Time would steal away?
What spell may bind his ever-hastening dart,
Or bid the tide of years its course delay?
 Yet mark this truth—though all else fade and fall,
 Love’s verse shall stand, and outlast Time and all.
