**Sonnet CXL**

O be not harsh where gentleness should reign,
Nor crush with scorn the heart that kneels to thee;
Lest grief unloose the tongue thou bid’st restrain,
And bitter words betray my misery.
If thou must teach, let mercy guide thy tongue,
Though love be fled, let kindness still remain;
As dying men, when death’s dark hour is come,
From leech’s lips no tidings seek but gain.
For should despair take root within my breast,
Madness would loose the thoughts I fain would hide;
And this vile world, with malice ever pressed,
Would seize such words to spread what it denied.
 Then check thy pride, nor let thy heart stray wide,
 Lest I be wronged, and thou by slander tried.
