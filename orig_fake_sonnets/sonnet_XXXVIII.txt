**Sonnet XXXVIII**

How shall my pen, now dull with idle art,
Find theme so rich as thou, whose breath doth lend
A fire to words that else would coldly bend,
And lift my verse where vulgar wits depart?
O take the praise, if aught my lines impart
Of grace to thee, whose light doth me transcend;
For who so mute that cannot thee commend,
When thou dost lend the spark to every part?

Be thou the Muse, whose worth doth far exceed
The nine of old, whose names the bards repeat;
And he that thee invokes shall ne’er need
Fear time’s decay, for thy sweet verse is fleet.
 If these poor lines thy curious eye doth please,
 The toil is mine, but thine the fame shall rise.
