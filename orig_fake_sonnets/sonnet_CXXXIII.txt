**Sonnet CXXXIII**

O cursed chain that binds my soul to thee,
And drags my dearest friend into thy snare!
What fiend hath wrought this double misery,
To make of love a torment past compare?
Thy glance hath stolen the self I held most true,
And made of him a captive to thy will;
Now torn from both, I know not what to do—
A grief so vast it doth my senses spill.

O lock my heart within thy iron breast,
But free his own, lest I in anguish die!
Let him be guard where’er my soul finds rest,
And spare me then thy cruelest tyranny.
 Yet still thou wilt not; for thy power is such
 That all I am, and all I love, is thine as much.
