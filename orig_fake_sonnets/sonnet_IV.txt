**Sonnet IV**

O fleeting grace, why hoard thy golden store,
When time doth tax thee for each borrowed ray?
The gift was lent, not given to thee for more
Than to bestow, and thus prolong thy sway.
Yet thou, like some vain miser, dost withhold
The wealth that should in others’ eyes take root,
And in thy chest of ivory and gold,
Dost starve the world to glut thy barren suit.

What boots it thee to guard a fading prize,
If none shall praise the bloom thou dost deny?
The rose that shuts its heart from summer skies
Must wither, unremembered, when it dies.
Then spend, fair fool, ere winter’s breath be near—
Thy light, unshared, is but a shadow’s bier.
