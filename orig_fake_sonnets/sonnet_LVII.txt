**Sonnet LVII**

O bound in chains of love’s unyielding might,
What task remains but to await thy call?
No golden hour escapes my watchful sight,
Nor duty stirs until thou bid’st it all.
No bitter hour of absence dares complain,
When thou, my sun, dost bid the shadows rise;
Nor jealous thought shall dare to probe thy reign,
Nor ask what joys in other’s fortune lies.
Like some pale ghost, I linger, dumb and meek,
And count the moments till thy face I see—
No thought but this my weary spirit seeks:
How bright thy light must shine where’er thou be.
 So love doth blind, that though thou break each vow,
 I kiss the rod that strikes me—foolish now.
