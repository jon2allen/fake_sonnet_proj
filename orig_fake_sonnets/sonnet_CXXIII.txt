**Sonnet CXXIII**

O fleeting Time, thy vaunted art is vain—
Thy gilded towers and monuments of dust
Are but the shadows of a ghostly reign,
Where pride and pomp dissolve in time’s just thrust.
The ages turn, yet in my steadfast breast
No change doth stir, no fickle whim prevail;
What thou dost build, I scorn as hollow jest,
For truth outlives the lies thy haste doth hail.

Thy scrolls of years, thy boasts of fleeting might,
Are but the echoes of a hollow sound;
I spurn thy records, forged in shifting light,
And stand unbowed where lesser men are bound.
This vow I keep, though all the world decay—
My soul is fixed, and scorns thy scythe’s array.
