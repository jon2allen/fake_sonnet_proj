**Sonnet LXVIII**

Behold his face, where time hath left its trace,
Not gilded o’er with art’s deceitful hand,
Nor robbed of youth to grace another’s face,
Nor decked in hues by cunning’s false command.
No stolen locks from tombs in golden guise
Adorn his brow, nor borrowed bloom bestow,
But nature’s truth in unadorned guise
Doth on his features softly come and go.
As maps of old did show the unspoiled earth,
Ere man’s bold touch did mar the sacred ground,
So stands he, pure in undefiled worth,
A living chart where time’s true lines are found.
 O let him be the glass wherein we see
 What beauty was ere art’s false alchemy.
