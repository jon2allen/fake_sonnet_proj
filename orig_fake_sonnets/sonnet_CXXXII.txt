**Sonnet CXXXII**

Thy gaze, a star in midnight’s velvet deep,
Doth pierce my breast with sorrow’s sharpened dart,
Yet in that shade where pity’s shadows weep,
I find the light that binds my wounded heart.
No golden dawn nor twilight’s fading hue
Could match the grace thy sable mourning shows,
For beauty dwells where darkness claims its due,
And love in grief more brightly, purely glows.

Then let thy soul, as fair as thy dark eyes,
With tender ruth my anguish now embrace,
And let thy pity in kind measure rise,
To match the grace that time cannot erase.
Thus shall I swear no fairer hue exists,
Than black where love in mournful splendor twists.
