**Sonnet CXXI**

Why bow I to the scorn of hollow gaze,
When truth within doth burn unsoiled, untamed?
Let envious tongues their venomed judgments raise—
My soul stands firm, by no false light inflamed.
What though the world with jaundiced eyes behold
My acts as stains upon a tarnished name?
Their hearts are ledgers where their own sins are told,
And in my faults they trace their guilt and shame.

I walk my path, though crooked they call straight,
And in their censure find my freedom’s song.
Let them in shadows nurse their dark debate—
My light needs not their dim approval’s throng.
 For if all men must bend to scorn’s decree,
 Then scorn itself is all the world shall see.
