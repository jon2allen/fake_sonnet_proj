**Sonnet CXXXIV**

Thus do I yield, bound fast in love’s harsh chain,
And pledge my heart to thee, a debt unpaid,
Yet crave but this—his freedom to regain,
Whom thou dost hold in bonds by me betrayed.
But thou, relentless, wilt not loose thy grip,
Nor shall he break the vow that chains him near;
For gold thou covet’st, and he, soft of lip,
Hath sworn to stand my surety, though in fear.

O cruel usurer! Thy beauty’s law
Demands all coin, all comfort, all delight.
Thou call’st my friend to pay what I did draw,
And so by thee I lose him to thy might.
   Him have I lost; thou hast both him and me:
   He pays the debt, yet still I am not free.
