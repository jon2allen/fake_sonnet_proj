**Sonnet CXII**

O let thy grace my tarnished name restore,
As morning sun doth melt the frost’s cold trace;
What reck I though the world should scorn or roar,
If thou dost clasp me in thy sweet embrace?
Thou art my heaven, my earth, my sun, my star,
And from thy lips alone my worth is spun;
No other voice can wound me near or far,
Nor I regard what malice hath begun.
So deep I sink in love’s unmeasured sea,
That scorn and praise alike are drowned in thee.

Lo, how I cast all heed of tongues away,
For thou dost reign within my heart’s own throne;
So fixed in thee, the world’s dull clamor may
Fade as a dream when waking light is shown.
