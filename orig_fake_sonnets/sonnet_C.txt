**Sonnet C (Composite)**

O fleeting sprite, why dost thou linger now,
When golden hours in idle silence fade?
Dost thou thy fire on trifles vain bestow,
And gild with art what merit never made?
Awake, my Muse, and shake thy drowsy wing,
Let not the hours in barren sloth be lost;
Sing thou of love, that time may cease to sting,
And bid thy verse outlast the frost’s harsh cost.

Behold, where beauty’s lamp doth softly burn,
If Time hath left his mark upon that face,
Then let thy song his cruel theft adjourn,
And build in rhyme what years cannot erase.
 So shall my love in thy sweet lines survive,
 And scorn the scythe that cuts all else alive.
