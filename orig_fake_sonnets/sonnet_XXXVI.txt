**Sonnet XXXVI**

O must we part, though bound by love’s strong chain,
And walk alone where once we trod as one?
The weight I bear is mine alone to sustain,
For time and fate have rent what love had spun.
Yet in our hearts no severance can divide,
Though hands may loose what hands once intertwined;
No spite of life can love’s true form deride,
Yet steals the joy that should our souls entwine.

I dare not name thee where the world may see,
Lest shadows cling where once thy light did shine;
Nor mayst thou lift me where all eyes may be,
Unless thou rend the grace that once was thine.
 Yet do not so—my heart holds thee too dear,
 For thine own fame is mine while thou art near.
