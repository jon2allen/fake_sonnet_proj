**Sonnet CXVI**

O let no bar the steadfast heart divide,
Nor fleeting storm nor fortune’s fickle hand
Unseat the love that stands on surer ground—
A flame unquenched where time and tide abide.
Not love the shifting sand, nor love the tide
That ebbs with doubt or swells with vain command,
But fixed as stars that guide the lost to land,
Though heaven’s own wheel in ceaseless circles glide.

O no! It scorns the reaper’s cruel decree,
Nor bows to years that wither bloom and grace,
But burns beyond the reach of fate’s decree,
A light no shadow of the grave can chase.
 If this be false, and love prove but a name,
 Then all my verse is dust, and love’s a flame.
