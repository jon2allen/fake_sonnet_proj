**Sonnet XXXIV**

Why dost thou swear the skies shall ever shine,
And bid me walk unburdened by thy vow,
When sudden storms in murky shrouds entwine,
And steal the light that erst did on me glow?
Thy tardy sun, though late it pierce the gloom,
Doth scant repair the harm thy faith hath wrought—
A balm too faint to cleanse the wound’s deep tomb,
Nor wash the stain thy broken troth hath brought.
No penitence can mend what thou hast marred,
Nor tears restore the trust thy hand did slay;
The weight of wrongs too fiercely borne is scarred,
And sorrow’s sighs but mock the debt I pay.
 Yet lo! thy grief, like pearls in sorrow strung,
 Doth buy my peace, though late thy heart be wrung.
