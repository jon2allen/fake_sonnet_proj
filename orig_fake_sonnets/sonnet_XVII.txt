**Sonnet XVII**

What hand shall dare to carve thy praise in stone,
When time itself must falter at thy name?
For ink is weak, and parchment but a groan
Where mortal verse would strive to match thy flame.
If I should paint thee in the hues of dawn,
Or trace thy grace in stars’ unyielding light,
The world would scoff and call my labor gone,
A fool’s vain dream to cage the infinite.

Yet let them mock—let yellowed pages fade,
Let tongues of scorn outlive my trembling line.
For though my words may wither in the shade,
Thy truth shall burn where lesser lights decline.
 But should thy blood in kindred veins yet run,
 Thou liv’st anew—in them, and in my son.
