**Sonnet LIII**

O fleeting form, what essence dost thou bear,
That all the world in borrowed hues must shine?
Each mimic shape, each shadowed grace compare,
Yet none can hold the light that is but thine.
Paint Venus’ face, or trace the rose’s bloom—
Thy loveliness outstrips the feigned art;
No gold of dawn, no summer’s sweet perfume
Can match the fire that burns within thy heart.
The poet’s quill, the painter’s cunning hand,
Strive but to catch the glimmer of thy might;
Yet all their toil, though wrought with skill so grand,
Fades ‘gainst the sun of thy unchanging light.
 In every grace some fragment thou dost lend,
 But none like thee—none, love, can thee transcend.
