**Sonnet CXLVI**

O weary ghost, bound in this clay’s dark hold,
Why gild thy cage with vanity’s bright chain?
Dost thou not see how fleeting is thy gold,
How time will rend thy gilded spoil in twain?
Why starve thy light to feast this dust’s desire,
When worms shall sup where thou hast spent thy breath?
Turn inward then, and quench thy soul’s own fire,
Let flesh consume itself in wanton death.

For what avails the pomp of mortal days,
When all must fade like embers in the wind?
Nay, feed thy spirit on the world’s decay,
And let the body’s pride be thine to chide.
Thus shalt thou mock the grave that claims thee then—
And Death, once slain, shall die, nor rise again.
