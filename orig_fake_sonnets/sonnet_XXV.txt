**Sonnet XXV**

Let those who chase the fleeting crown of fame,
Or ride the gilded tide of fortune’s grace,
Boast loud their names in time’s unkind acclaim—
I scorn such toys, and seek a sweeter place.
The courtier’s smile, like April’s fickle sun,
May lift the rose, yet leaves it pale and spent;
The soldier’s laurel, hard by valor won,
Fades when one blow undoes his hard-won bent.

O, let them toil for titles, gold, or might—
I ask no spoils where love and truth abide.
No storm may shake, no time may dim the light
That burns where two in steadfast faith reside.
 Then blest am I, who love and am beloved,
 And in that bond all other joys are proved.
