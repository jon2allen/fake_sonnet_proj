**Sonnet LIX**

If time doth wear all shapes in endlesse round,
And nought is born but what hath walked ere,
Why fret our souls, by toil in labour bound,
To forge fresh chains where ancient links appear?
O had I but some scroll of elder days,
When quill first kissed the page with passion’s flame,
To spy thy form in faded, yellowed lays,
And trace the lineaments of love’s first claim!
Then might I judge if Nature’s hand did mend,
Or if the golden age did bear the palm,
Or if, in ceaseless turn, all things descend
To dust, yet rise in shapes no less but calm.
   Yet this I know—though lesser themes they chose,
   The bards of old did sing with hearts as bold as those.
