**Sonnet IX**

Why dost thou hoard thy days in barren pride,
And scorn the gift that time doth bid thee give?
The world shall mourn thee when thou art denied
The tender pledge by which thy name shall live.
For what are years but shadows swiftly flown,
If none remain to bear thy face anew?
The widow weeps, yet in her child is shown
The form that death would else have stolen from view.
O reckless soul! What treasure dost thou keep
That fades not with thee when thy breath is done?
Beauty must bloom or wither in the deep,
And love unshared is but a stolen sun.
 Then let thy heart not shrink from love’s sweet cost—
 Lest all thy light be lost, and thou be lost.
