**Sonnet CL**

O cruel art! What alchemy is this,
That turns my soul to wax beneath thy flame?
Thou paint’st the night with hues of morning’s kiss,
And bid’st me call foul darkness by love’s name.
From whence dost steal the fire that gilds thy wrong,
That even thy faults in splendor stand arrayed?
What sorcerer’s tongue, so sweet, so false, so long,
Hath taught thee how to make my heart betrayed?

For thou dost weave such webs of cunning guile,
That hate itself is lost in thy deceit;
And though the world should scorn thee all the while,
I, wretch, must cling to what they all repeat.
 Yet if my love from thy unworthiness rise,
 Then worthier thou to claim my sacrifice.
