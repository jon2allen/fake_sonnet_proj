**Sonnet CXXXIX**

O! cease thy silent war, nor twist the knife
That turns within my breast with cruel delay;
Speak thou the truth, though bitter as thy strife,
And let thy words my lingering death betray.
Why veil thy scorn in glances sharp as steel,
When speech might strike and end my tormented state?
Thy tongue, though harsh, would mercy’s office deal,
Where artful looks but mock my hapless fate.
Yet if thou must, then let thy eyes confess
What lips refuse—let them my doom decree;
No more this dance of half-avowed distress,
No more this war ‘twixt love and treachery.
   Then kill me now, nor leave me thus undone,
   But strike me down with one unflinching sun.
