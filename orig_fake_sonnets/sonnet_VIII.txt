**Sonnet VIII**

O harmony! Why dost thou strike me dumb,
When every note should set my spirit free?
Thy concord weaves a bond no hand may sunder,
Yet I, alone, stand mute where all agree.

Behold the lute, where strings in love entwine,
Each trembling touch doth answer, soft and clear;
So father, child, and mother sweetly chime,
A trinity of sound, yet one voice near.

Why then dost thou, who hear’st this blissful art,
Turn from the choir that bids thee join the throng?
Thou hold’st apart what should be knit in song—
A single string, untuned, breaks every part.

 Then heed the lesson that the music shows:
 *"No life is whole that in itself still goes."*
