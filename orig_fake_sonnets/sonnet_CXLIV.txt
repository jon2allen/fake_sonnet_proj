**Sonnet CXLIV**

Two flames within my breast do wage their war,
One bright as dawn, the other dark as night:
The first, a star that guides me evermore,
The last, a shadow born of foul delight.
O cruel enchantress, clad in serpent’s guise,
Thou steal’st my light with thy bewitching art,
And seek’st to drag my virtue to the skies—
Or plunge it deep where sin and shame impart.
Yet whether heaven or hell shall claim my soul,
I dare not say, though doubt doth gnaw my mind;
For when from both my heart doth lose control,
I fear the worst in love’s dark maze confined.
 This doubt shall haunt me till the end be near,
 When one shall quench the other—then appear.
