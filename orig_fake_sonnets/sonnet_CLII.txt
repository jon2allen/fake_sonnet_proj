**Sonnet CLII**

O love, thou know’st my heart is bound in chains,
Yet thou dost break the vows that thou hast sworn,
And in thy breast new fires and new disdains
Do rise where once devotion’s light was born.
But why dost thou accuse me of deceit,
When I have sworn a thousand oaths in vain?
For all my vows were but a fleeting heat,
And all my faith dissolved in bitter rain.

I swore thee kind, and constant as the sun,
And called thee true when truth was but a shade;
I made my eyes deny what they had known,
And bound my tongue to praise what thou hast made.
 Yet I am false—more false than thou couldst be,
 To swear thee fair when foulness dwells in thee.
