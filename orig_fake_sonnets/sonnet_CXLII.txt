**Sonnet CXLII**

O cruel delight! Thy scorn doth wound me deep,
Yet in thy pride my folly finds its grace.
What sin is mine, if love must wake and weep,
When thou dost walk in beauty’s cold embrace?
Compare thy heart to mine, and thou shalt see
No stain so black as that which marks thy name—
For thou hast sworn where none should bend the knee,
And sealed false vows with lips that feed on flame.

Yet love thee still I must, as thou dost crave
The hearts of those who tremble at thy sight.
Let mercy grow where now thy scorn is grave,
That pity’s seed may blossom into light.
 If thou wouldst hide what thou dost most desire,
 Then take my love, and quench thy heart’s own fire.
