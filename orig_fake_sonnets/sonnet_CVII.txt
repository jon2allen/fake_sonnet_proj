**Sonnet CVII**

No whispered dread, nor fate’s unyielding hand,
Nor time’s cold breath that gnaws the gilded frame,
Shall stay the pulse of love I here expand,
Nor chain the light that burns beyond its name.
The raven moon hath fled the ashen sky,
And seers now choke upon their hollow art;
What once was frail now stands in iron high,
And mercy writes her name on fortune’s chart.

O golden hour, whose dew revives the rose,
My love outstrips the worm that waits below,
For though the flesh must fade where winter grows,
These lines shall bloom where tyrants’ laurels go.
Thus in my verse thy glory shall endure,
When marble cracks and empires turn to dust.
