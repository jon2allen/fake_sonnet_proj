**Sonnet XCVIII**

When golden May did deck the laughing earth,
And painted fields in gaudy hues arrayed,
No wanton bloom could stir my heart to mirth,
Nor lark’s sweet song my frozen thoughts persuade.
The violet’s sigh, the primrose’s shy glance,
The daisy’s blush—all vain and hollow show—
For still my soul in barren winter’s trance
Did walk, where no true warmth was wont to glow.

O cruel deceit! That nature’s fairest face
Should mock my grief with borrowed summer’s light,
When all her charms but shadow forth thy grace,
And fade like dreams before the coming night.
 Yet though the world in borrowed splendor plays,
 My heart still mourns, and winter with thee stays.
