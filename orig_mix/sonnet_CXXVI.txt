**Sonnet CXXVI**

O fleeting form, whose grace doth mock the years,
As Time’s unkind glass steals thy bloom away,
Yet in thy waning, brighter still appears
The light that dims the dawn of lesser day.
What hand doth stay the scythe’s relentless sway?
What art delays the hour that must be paid?
Nature, who shaped thee for her brief display,
Now claims her debt—no ransom can be made.

Yet mark her cunning: though she bids thee rise,
She guards thee not from fate’s unyielding hand.
The ledger tallies, though she feigns disguise,
And all must yield to her unchanging command.
Then take thy crown, though short the reign may be,
For beauty fades, yet lives in memory.
