**Sonnet LXXXVII**

O parting breath! Too bright for hands so frail,
As stars must slip from fingers stretched in vain,
Thy light, self-known, doth mock my feeble chain,
And breaks the vow no mortal tongue may hale.
What hold I claim but what thy grace doth lend?
What merit mine could bid such splendor stay?
The gift was thine—now reason bids obey,
And back to thee the borrowed gold doth wend.

Thou gav’st thyself, nor knew’st thy worth’s true height,
Or me, unfit to bear such radiant load;
Thus error’s bloom must wither into night,
And wisdom call thee home from folly’s road.
 So did I grasp thee, as a fleeting spark—
 A king in dreams, in waking, naught but dark.
