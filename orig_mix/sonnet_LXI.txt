**Sonnet LXI**

Dost bid my weary lids to wakeful keep,
As though thy shade would haunt my restless bed?
Or dost thou send thy ghost my dreams to weep,
Where phantom loves my troubled thoughts have fed?
Nay, ‘tis not thou who steal’st my slumber’s grace,
Nor jealousy that spies my fleeting hours—
‘Tis love alone, that holds me in this place,
And binds my soul to watch thy distant bowers.
For though thou roam where other voices call,
And leave me lone to guard the silent night,
Yet still my heart doth stand sentinel for all,
A flame unquenched by absence or by slight.
 So wake I here, while thou dost wake afar,
 And love’s bright torch outburns the evening star.
