**Sonnet XX**

O form divine, where heaven’s art doth blend
A grace no mortal hand could e’er impart—
Not wholly man, nor woman at the end,
Yet both in one to captivate the heart.
Thy glance, a sun that gilds the darkest air,
Outshines the coy and fleeting eyes of maids;
Thy voice, a lute where sorrow finds repair,
With notes that time nor treason e’er invades.

First shaped for love’s sweet purpose, yet too fair,
Nature, in crafting thee, did lose her way,
And gave what steals both maid and man’s despair,
A gift that mocks the vows of yesterday.
 Then take this heart, though fashioned for her pleasure,
 And let her borrow what is mine by measure.
