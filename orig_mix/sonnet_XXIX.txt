**Sonnet XXIX**

When fortune’s frown doth weigh my spirit low,
And scornful eyes do mark my wretched state,
I pour my plaint where no ear heeds my woe,
And in despair my hapless lot berate.
O, how I long for hope’s bright golden store,
For comely grace, or friends to cheer my days,
To match this wit, or that man’s skill explore,
While joys I hold now fade in dull malaise.

Yet in this mire of self-reviling thought,
A sudden light doth pierce the gloom’s embrace—
Thy love, like dawn, my weary heart hath sought,
And lifts my soul to heaven’s own grace.
 For memory of thee doth riches bring,
 And makes me scorn all earthly crowns to cling.
