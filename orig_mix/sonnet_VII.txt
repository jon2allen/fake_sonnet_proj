**Sonnet VII**

Behold! when first the dawn doth break in gold,
And lifts her torch to wake the slumb’ring earth,
Each weary eye doth greet her light untold,
And pays sweet tribute to her morning’s birth.
She climbs the sky with youth’s unwearied grace,
Yet time shall bend her to his cruel decree,
And still men gaze upon her fleeting face,
As she doth trace her path of majesty.

But when at last her strength begins to fade,
And age doth drag her down the western steep,
The eyes that once did watch her bright parade
Now turn away and let her sink to sleep.
 So thou, who shin’st in life’s unclouded hour,
 Must fade unseen—save by some fruit of power.
