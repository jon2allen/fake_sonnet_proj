**Sonnet XVI**

O why not seize the reins of fleeting days,
And bid stern Time his ravages delay?
Let not his scythe your golden hours betray,
Nor trust to verse alone your fame’s bright blaze.
Lo, youth’s fair bloom, like roses newly met,
Doth beg your seed in gardens yet untrod,
Where living blooms shall rise, more bright, more odd,
Than art’s cold hues or my poor lines beset.
For lines of blood, not ink, shall life restore,
And flesh outlast the shadow Time hath cast—
No painted cheek nor pen’s weak art shall last,
But heirs shall bear your fire to distant shore.
 Then give yourself, and thus your self retain,
 For time may steal, but blood shall still remain.
