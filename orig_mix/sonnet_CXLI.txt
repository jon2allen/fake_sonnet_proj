**Sonnet CXLI**

Not with the gaze that spies thy fleeting grace,
Nor by the ear that hears thy hollow song,
Nor touch that trembles at thy wanton wrong,
Nor scent nor savor that thy charms embrace—
Yet still my soul, enslaved by thy dark face,
Defies the senses’ counsel, wise and strong,
And clings to thee, though reason bids me throng
From thee, whose pride doth mock my lowly place.

O cruel delight! O torment sweetly spun!
My wit, my will, my every part doth plead
In vain—thy slave I am, undone, undone.
Yet this I count my meed, my sole meed:
That she who leads me to this sinful deed
Doth crown my pain with pain, my loss with none.
