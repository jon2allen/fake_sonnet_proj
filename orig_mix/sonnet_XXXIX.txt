**Sonnet XXXIX**

How shall my trembling quill thy grace portray,
When thou dost dwell within my inmost part?
What verse can frame thy praise, yet not betray
The debt my soul owes to thy gentle art?
Let distance then our mingled hearts divide,
That I may sing what else were left unsung—
Not love entire, but half in thee abide,
And half in echoes of thy spirit hung.
O cruel lack! Yet sweet thy absence proves,
For in thy void my thoughts to thee take flight,
And weave such dreams as time itself approves,
Turning each hour to gold, each day to night.
Thus thou dost teach me how to rend in twain,
And praise thee here, though thou dost far remain.
