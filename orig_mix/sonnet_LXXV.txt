**Sonnet LXXV**

As fire to frost, or dawn unto the night,
So art thou woven in my waking mind—
A flame that burns, yet leaves no mark behind,
A light that fades but never quite takes flight.
Now bold as kings who clutch their crowns so tight,
Now trembling as the leaf before the wind,
I hoard thy glance, yet fear the hours unkind
Shall steal the gold I guard with such delight.

Oft drunk upon the nectar of thy face,
Then parched as deserts long for rain’s embrace,
I feast, I starve—no middle path is found.
No joy but thine, no sorrow but thy grace,
No heaven but where thy shadow may be traced,
No hell but where thy absence wraps me round.
