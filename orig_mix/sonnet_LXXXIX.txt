**Sonnet LXXXIX**

If thou shouldst charge me with some grievous crime,
I’ll bear the weight and call it justly mine;
Name but my flaw, and I shall not repine,
But bow my head and let thy scorn run rife.
No wound thou giv’st could match the pain I’d give,
To twist my love to hate, my joy to strife,
For knowing well thy will, I’ll cease to live
As once I did, and let my heart grow stiff.

No more shall I thy paths in secret tread,
Nor speak thy name with lips once sworn to thee,
Lest I profane what time has left unsaid,
Or wake the ghost of what we used to be.
 For thee, I’ll war with all my soul’s delight,
 And kill the love thou bid’st me put to flight.
