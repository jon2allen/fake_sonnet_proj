**Sonnet CV**

O call not mine a worship vain and blind,
Nor deem my love a graven shape of stone,
For though my verse doth weave one theme alone,
Yet in that one all difference I find.
Not fickle as the moon’s inconstant light,
Nor cold as marble, nor as flame untrue,
But steadfast as the star that guides the night,
And bright as dawn, and tender as the dew.
“Loyal, and bright, and kind,” my muse doth sing,
“Loyal, and bright, and kind,” in endless rhyme,
For in these three my weary pen takes wing,
And finds in one the scope of endless time.
 Loyal, and bright, and kind—each stood apart,
 Till now in thee they blend one perfect heart.
