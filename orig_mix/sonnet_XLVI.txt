**Sonnet XLVI**

O cruel strife that rends my soul in twain,
Where eye and heart contend for thy sweet grace,
One claims thy form, the other thy heart’s trace,
Yet neither yields, nor bends to love’s sweet pain.
The eye doth swear thy beauty is its gain,
A sight no time nor distance can erase,
While heart doth plead thy love’s unchanging place,
A flame no outward glance could e’er restrain.

Then let the mind, that arbiter so wise,
Judge betwixt these two with even hand,
And weigh their claims with reason’s steadfast eyes.
For thee, my love, no partial doom shall stand—
The eye shall keep thy fair and fleeting guise,
The heart thy soul, where true affection lies.
